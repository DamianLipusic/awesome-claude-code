# Mobile-First Multiplayer Business/Economy Game — MVP Specification

---

## 1. FINAL MVP GAME LOOP

### New Player Onboarding (Day 0–1)

1. Player registers, selects a starting city (shared server, 3–5 regional markets)
2. Tutorial grants $5,000 seed capital and one Tier-1 business slot
3. Player chooses a starting vertical: Retail, Manufacturing, or Extraction
4. First 30 minutes: buy resources from AI market, set up shop, make first sale
5. System introduces the Employee tab at 10 employees hired
6. Player receives a "Business License" item — unlocks contracts and player marketplace

### Early Growth (Week 1–3)

- Player operates 1–2 businesses, hiring NPCs, buying from AI market
- Discovers player market offers better bulk pricing
- Forms first trade contract with another player (or accepts an open contract)
- Accumulates enough capital to expand: second business slot unlocks at $50,000 net worth
- Optional: player encounters a Criminal Opportunity event (can ignore or engage)

### Mid-Game (Month 1–4)

- Player operates 3–8 business entities across a supply chain
- Relies heavily on player market for inputs and outputs
- Manages employee loyalty/corruption risk actively
- Legal path: invests in Security Layer, wins supply contracts, builds vertical integration
- Criminal path: runs parallel dirty operations, launders through legitimate businesses, manages Heat Score
- Both paths: engages in competitive sabotage (legal path through market cornering, criminal path through direct ops)
- Season leaderboard becomes visible at Month 2 — creates competitive pressure

### Late Game (Month 4–6)

- Top players control significant portions of one or more resource categories
- Anti-snowball mechanics activate (progressive taxation, market exposure caps)
- Criminal players face elevated detection risk as Heat Score rises
- Alliance/rivalry dynamics reach peak tension
- Season end announced 2 weeks prior — players race for leaderboard position
- Final 48 hours: "Endgame Rush" — special high-risk contracts and crime ops available

### Season End / Wipe

- Final standings locked, achievements distributed
- Meta-progression rewards granted (cosmetics, small permanent bonuses — see Season System)
- Full economy wipe: cash, businesses, employees, resources reset
- Player accounts persist: username, meta-progression, achievement history
- New season starts with slight rule variant (announced 1 week prior)

## 2. CORE SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

### System Dependency Map

```
SeasonProfile
    └── governs → [Player, Business, Market, Economy]

Player
    ├── owns → Business[]
    ├── holds → Resource[] (inventory)
    ├── has → HeatScore
    ├── has → DirtyMoneyBalance
    └── has → SecurityLayer

Business
    ├── employs → Employee[]
    ├── produces/consumes → Resource[]
    ├── lists on → MarketListing[]
    ├── participates in → TradeContract[]
    └── can run → CriminalOperation[]

MarketListing
    ├── AI System Listings (generated by economy_update())
    └── Player Listings (created by Player/Business)

TradeContract
    ├── links → Business ↔ Business (or Business ↔ AI)
    └── references → Resource, price, quantity, schedule

CriminalOperation
    ├── generates → DirtyMoneyBalance
    ├── triggers → detection_check()
    └── modifies → HeatScore

DirtyMoneyBalance
    └── enters → LaunderingProcess[]

LaunderingProcess
    ├── uses → Business (as cover)
    ├── incurs → fees, time delay
    └── outputs → clean Player cash

SecurityLayer
    ├── protects → Business (from robbery, sabotage)
    └── reduces → corruption risk on Employee[]

HeatScore
    ├── influences → detection_check() probability
    ├── influenced by → CriminalOperation frequency
    └── decays → over time (inactivity, bribes)
```

### Simulation Loop Architecture

```
Every 5 minutes (tick):
    economy_update()          → update AI market prices and quantities
    employee_production()     → each Business produces based on employees
    laundering_tick()         → advance LaunderingProcess timers
    detection_check()         → evaluate criminal exposure
    contract_settlement()     → settle due TradeContracts

Every 1 hour:
    market_refresh()          → replenish AI market stock, apply player price pressure
    heat_decay()              → reduce HeatScore by base decay rate

Every 24 hours:
    daily_costs()             → deduct rent, employee salaries, loan interest
    progressive_tax()         → apply wealth-bracket taxation
    season_milestone_check()  → award milestone badges
```

### Inter-System Data Flow

```
Raw Material (Resource) → Business (Factory) → Processed Good (Resource)
    ↑ AI Market or TradeContract              ↓ MarketListing or TradeContract
                                          Player B purchases → their Business → ...

CriminalOperation → DirtyMoney → LaunderingProcess → Clean Cash → Business Reinvestment
                            ↓
                      HeatScore rises → detection_check() → Penalty or Clear
```

## 4. MARKET LOGIC

### AI Market vs Player Market Coexistence

The economy has two parallel market layers that coexist within each city's marketplace screen.

**AI Market (Layer A) — Always On Baseline**

The AI market simulates a floor of economic activity. It exists so new players can always buy/sell without needing another human online.

Pricing formula:

```
ai_sell_price(resource) = resource.base_value * (1 + supply_scarcity_factor) * ai_markup

supply_scarcity_factor = max(0, (target_supply - global_supply) / target_supply)
ai_markup = 1.25  // AI always sells 25% above base value

ai_buy_price(resource) = resource.base_value * 0.75  // AI buys at 25% below base
```

Quantity caps per city per market refresh (hourly):

```
ai_quantity_cap(resource, city) = base_cap * city_size_modifier
    base_cap: Tier-1 resources: 500 units, Tier-2: 200, Tier-3: 100, Tier-4: 50, Tier-5: 20
    city_size_modifier: Small=0.5, Medium=1.0, Large=1.5, Capital=2.0
```

The AI quantity cap resets hourly and does not accumulate. If players exhaust AI supply, they must use the player market or wait.

**Player Market (Layer B) — Emergent Competitive Layer**

Players post listings (buy or sell) with no mandatory markup. The system does not set player prices. Players can undercut, corner markets, form pricing cartels, or offer bulk discounts.

Player listing rules:
- Minimum listing: 1 unit, no maximum
- Listing fee: 2% of total listing value, paid upfront, non-refundable
- Listing duration: 24h, 72h, or 7 days (seller chooses)
- Anonymous listings: allowed, but costs an additional 1% fee
- Bulk minimums: seller can set a minimum purchase quantity

**Why Player Networking Wins**

The AI market has structural weaknesses the player market doesn't:
1. AI buy price is always 25% below base — players can offer 10–20% below base and still beat it
2. AI quantity caps mean high-volume players cannot source enough from AI
3. AI never offers TradeContracts — price certainty, volume guarantees, and locked-in delivery schedules are player-only
4. Player market prices fluctuate based on world events; a savvy player who locks in a contract before a scarcity event gains massive margin
5. AI market never buys illegal resources — criminal players must use the player market (or launder first)

**Price Discovery Mechanics**

The market screen shows:
- Current AI price (always visible)
- Lowest player ask, highest player bid (visible to all)
- 24-hour price history chart
- Volume traded in last 24 hours

This transparency creates real price discovery — players can see when the player market is better than the AI baseline.

**Cross-City Logistics**

Resources listed in City A cannot be instantly used in City B. Moving inventory between cities:
- Requires assigning a LOGISTICS business or paying per-unit transport fee
- Transport time: 1–4 hours depending on distance
- Transport cost: $0.05–0.20 per unit per city-hop
- Criminal resources: cannot be shipped via standard logistics (must use criminal network)

This creates genuine geographic market segmentation — prices vary by city.

## 5. CRIME DESIGN

### Criminal Path Overview

Crime is an alternative acceleration path, not a guaranteed win. It provides higher ceilings but introduces existential risk (asset seizure, business shutdown, market bans). A full criminal empire is harder to build and maintain than a legal one.

### Dirty Money Mechanics

All criminal operations yield dirty money, which is separate from clean cash. Dirty money:
- Cannot be used to buy on the standard player market
- Cannot pay employee salaries directly (would raise suspicion instantly)
- Cannot be used to purchase new Business slots
- CAN be used to pay for criminal services (enforcers, bribes, criminal network access)
- CAN be converted to clean cash via laundering

Dirty money sitting in DirtyMoneyBalance.total_dirty accumulates a 0.5% per day "liability fee" — holding dirty money costs you, incentivizing active laundering.

### Laundering Methods

Four laundering channels, each with different fee, speed, and detection risk:

```
BUSINESS_REVENUE
    fee: 15%
    duration: 48 hours per $10,000
    detection_risk_modifier: 0.8x (lower — blends naturally)
    requirement: Must own a RETAIL or FRONT_COMPANY business
    max_per_day: business.front_capacity

REAL_ESTATE (abstracted as "property investment")
    fee: 25%
    duration: 7 days per $50,000
    detection_risk_modifier: 0.5x (lowest — hard to trace)
    requirement: Net worth > $500,000 (unlocks at midgame)
    max_per_day: $100,000

SHELL_COMPANY
    fee: 30%
    duration: 24 hours per $25,000
    detection_risk_modifier: 1.0x (standard)
    requirement: Must own a FRONT_COMPANY business
    max_per_day: $50,000

CRYPTO_ANALOG (in-game "CipherCoin")
    fee: 10%
    duration: 6 hours per $5,000
    detection_risk_modifier: 2.0x (high — traceable if flagged)
    requirement: None (always available, but risky)
    max_per_day: $20,000
```

### Detection Probability Formula

The detection check runs every 5 minutes for each player with an active CriminalOperation or LaunderingProcess.

```
base_detection_rate = 0.02  // 2% per check baseline

detection_probability = base_detection_rate
    * heat_multiplier(player.heat_score.score)
    * operation_risk_multiplier(op.risk_level)
    * laundering_method_modifier
    * (1 - security_reduction)
    * (1 + informant_bonus)
    - bribe_discount

heat_multiplier(score):
    COLD (0–99):    1.0
    WARM (100–299): 1.5
    HOT (300–599):  2.5
    BURNING (600–899): 4.0
    FUGITIVE (900–1000): 6.0

operation_risk_multiplier:
    risk_level 1–3: 0.5
    risk_level 4–6: 1.0
    risk_level 7–9: 2.0
    risk_level 10:  3.5

laundering_method_modifier: (from laundering method above)

security_reduction = min(0.40, player.security_layer.protection_rating / 250)
    // max 40% reduction from security

informant_bonus = 0.50 if heat_score.informant_active else 0
    // an active informant doubles base risk

bribe_discount = 0.30 if bribe recently paid else 0
    // one-time 30% reduction, costs $10,000–$100,000 based on heat level
```

Detection check result roll:

```
roll = random(0, 1)
if roll < detection_probability:
    trigger_detection_event(player, operation)
```

### Detection Events (Tiered Outcomes)

Not all detections are catastrophic. Outcome depends on heat level:

```
COLD heat on detection:
    → "Close Call" — warning notification, Heat +20, no other penalty

WARM heat on detection:
    → "Investigation Opened" — Heat +50, business randomly audited (operations paused 6h)

HOT heat on detection (roll for severity):
    → 40% chance: Fine ($50,000–$200,000), Heat +80
    → 40% chance: Business Raid — one business seized/suspended 24h, inventory confiscated
    → 20% chance: Arrest — player account suspended 12h, Heat +200, assets partially frozen

BURNING heat on detection:
    → 30% chance: Major Raid — all criminal businesses suspended 48h, dirty money seized
    → 50% chance: Arrest — 24h suspension, 30% of assets seized
    → 20% chance: Full Takedown — all businesses suspended 72h, 60% assets seized, Heat reset to 100

FUGITIVE heat on detection:
    → Automatic Full Takedown every time
```

### Heat Score Management

Heat naturally decays:
```
decay_per_hour = max(1, score * 0.005)  // 0.5% of current score per hour, minimum 1 point
// At score 1000: decays ~5 points/hour = ~8 days to reach HOT
// At score 300: decays ~2 points/hour = ~6 days to reach COLD
```

Players can accelerate heat reduction:
- **Lay Low**: Stop all criminal ops for 24h → 2x decay rate
- **Bribe Officials**: Pay $10k–$100k → instant -100 Heat, 7-day cooldown
- **Use Informant on Rival**: Redirect 50 Heat to a rival player (criminal cooperation mechanic)

### Criminal Cooperation

Players can form a Criminal Network (analogous to legal trade partnerships):
- Up to 5 players in a network
- Shared heat pool is NOT implemented (each player keeps individual heat — prevents griefing)
- Network benefits: share criminal ops (higher yield, shared employee pool), access to higher-tier crime ops requiring multiple players
- Network betrayal mechanic: one player can "snitch" on network members — each member gets +200 Heat, snitch gets -200 Heat and a "Rat" reputation badge that increases all future NPC prices by 15%

## 6. BALANCE RULES

### Legal vs Crime Balance Targets

The design intent: legal path should win long-term strategy at scale; crime should win short-term acceleration with high risk. A pure criminal empire should be harder to sustain than a legal one.

```
Expected Net Worth at Season End (Target):
    Top legal player:    $10,000,000 – $30,000,000
    Top criminal player: $5,000,000 – $20,000,000 (higher ceiling but more losses from busts)
    Average player:      $200,000 – $1,000,000
```

### Anti-Snowball Mechanics

**Progressive Taxation (Daily, Auto-Applied)**

```
Net Worth Bracket    Tax Rate (daily)
$0 – $50,000         0%
$50,001 – $200,000   0.05% (very mild, barely noticed)
$200,001 – $500,000  0.15%
$500,001 – $2,000,000  0.30%
$2,000,001 – $10,000,000  0.60%
$10,000,001+         1.20% (significant drain at top)
```

Tax is applied to net worth, not just cash — forcing large players to keep capital working.

**Market Exposure Caps**

No single player can control more than:
- 40% of total player market listings for any single resource in any single city
- 3 of the same business type in any single city (prevents full monopoly)
- 25% of total active TradeContracts for any single resource globally

If a player exceeds these caps through organic growth, new listings are blocked until they fall below the threshold. This is displayed as "Market Saturation Warning" in the UI.

**Employee Scarcity Scaling**

Employee hiring cost increases as a player scales:

```
hiring_cost_modifier = 1 + (business_count * 0.15) + (employee_count / 100)
// 10 businesses + 50 employees = 1 + 1.5 + 0.5 = 3x base hiring cost
```

This makes large empires proportionally more expensive to staff.

**Rival Mechanics (Legal)**

Legal players can:
- File "Market Complaint" — if upheld (random + reputation-gated), temporarily reduces rival's listing cap
- Undercut campaigns — coordinated undercutting, no systemic enforcement (purely player-driven)
- Poach employees — pay 2x salary to flip a rival's employee (their loyalty stat resists this)

### Cost Scaling Formulas

**Business Operating Cost**

```
daily_cost = base_cost(type, tier) * (1 + (total_player_businesses / 100))
// Slight global scaling as server economy grows
// Prevents cost being trivial at large scale
```

**Business Upgrade Costs**

```
upgrade_cost(tier) = base_upgrade * (tier ^ 2.5)
Tier 1→2: $25,000
Tier 2→3: $140,000
Tier 3→4: $500,000
Tier 4→5: $1,500,000
```

**Employee Salary Scaling**

```
base_salary(role) * (1 + combined_stats / 200)
// An employee with 80 efficiency + 90 loyalty costs 85% more than baseline
```

### Database Schema Notes

```
function economy_update(season_id):
    resources = db.query("SELECT * FROM resources WHERE season_id = ?", season_id)

    for each resource in resources:
        total_supply = db.query("""
            SELECT SUM(quantity) FROM market_listings
            WHERE resource_id = ? AND status = 'OPEN' AND listing_type IN ('AI_SELL', 'PLAYER_SELL')
        """, resource.id)

        total_demand = estimate_demand(resource, season_id)
        // demand estimated from active businesses that consume this resource

        scarcity = max(0, (TARGET_SUPPLY[resource.tier] - total_supply) / TARGET_SUPPLY[resource.tier])
        new_ai_price = resource.base_value * (1 + scarcity) * AI_MARKUP

        // Clamp price movement to ±20% per tick to prevent shock
        max_change = resource.current_ai_price * 0.20
        new_ai_price = clamp(new_ai_price,
            resource.current_ai_price - max_change,
            resource.current_ai_price + max_change)

        db.update("resources SET current_ai_price = ? WHERE id = ?", new_ai_price, resource.id)
```

### employee_production()

```
function employee_production(business_id):
    business = db.get_business(business_id)
    if business.status != 'ACTIVE': return

    employees = db.get_employees(business_id)
    if employees.length == 0: return

    avg_efficiency = average(employees.map(e => e.efficiency)) / 100.0
    avg_reliability = average(employees.map(e => e.reliability)) / 100.0
    present_employees = employees.filter(e => random() < avg_reliability)

    // No workers showed up
    if present_employees.length == 0: return

    effective_efficiency = avg_efficiency * (present_employees.length / employees.length)
    units_to_produce = floor(business.capacity * effective_efficiency)

    // Check input resource availability
    recipe = PRODUCTION_RECIPES[business.type][business.tier]
    for each input in recipe.inputs:
        available = business.inventory[input.resource_id] ?? 0
        if available < input.quantity_per_unit * units_to_produce:
            units_to_produce = floor(available / input.quantity_per_unit)

    if units_to_produce == 0: return

    // Deduct inputs
    for each input in recipe.inputs:
        deduct_inventory(business, input.resource_id, input.quantity_per_unit * units_to_produce)

    // Add output
    output_resource = recipe.output
    add_inventory(business, output_resource.resource_id, units_to_produce)

    // Corruption check
    for each employee in present_employees:
        if random() < (employee.corruption_risk / 1000.0):
            corruption_amount = floor(units_to_produce * 0.02)  // steal 2%
            deduct_inventory(business, output_resource.resource_id, corruption_amount)
            emit_alert(business.owner_id, "employee_theft", {employee_id, amount: corruption_amount})
```

### market_refresh()

```
function market_refresh(season_id):
    cities = get_all_cities()
    resources = db.get_all_resources(season_id)

    for each city in cities:
        for each resource in resources:
            cap = calculate_ai_cap(resource.tier, city.size)

            // Remove expired AI listings
            db.delete_where("market_listings",
                "city = ? AND resource_id = ? AND listing_type = 'AI_SELL' AND expires_at < NOW()",
                city.name, resource.id)

            // Count remaining AI supply in this city
            existing_ai = db.count_ai_supply(city.name, resource.id)

            // Refill to cap
            if existing_ai < cap:
                refill_quantity = cap - existing_ai
                db.insert("market_listings", {
                    listing_type: 'AI_SELL',
                    city: city.name,
                    resource_id: resource.id,
                    quantity: refill_quantity,
                    quantity_remaining: refill_quantity,
                    price_per_unit: resource.current_ai_price,
                    expires_at: NOW() + 2 hours,
                    status: 'OPEN'
                })

    // Apply player market pressure to AI price
    for each resource:
        avg_player_price = db.query("""
            SELECT AVG(price_per_unit) FROM market_listings
            WHERE resource_id = ? AND listing_type = 'PLAYER_SELL' AND status = 'OPEN'
        """, resource.id)

        if avg_player_price is not null:
            // If players are selling cheaper, reduce AI price slightly
            if avg_player_price < resource.current_ai_price:
                adjustment = (resource.current_ai_price - avg_player_price) * 0.10
                resource.current_ai_price -= adjustment
                db.update_ai_price(resource.id, resource.current_ai_price)
```

```
function crime_action_resolve(operation_id):
    op = db.get_operation(operation_id)
    player = db.get_player(op.player_id)
    heat = db.get_heat_score(op.player_id)

    // Check if detected before resolving
    was_detected = detection_check(player, op, heat)

    if was_detected:
        apply_detection_penalty(player, heat)
        op.status = 'BUSTED'
        op.was_detected = true
        op.dirty_money_yield = 0
        db.update_operation(op)
        emit_alert(player.id, "crime_detected", {op_id: op.id, penalty_applied: true})
        return

    // Operation succeeded
    yield = calculate_yield(op)
    db.increment_dirty_money(player.id, yield)

    // Heat always increases on any criminal action, detected or not
    heat_gain = op.risk_level * 10
    db.increment_heat(player.id, heat_gain)

    op.status = 'COMPLETED'
    op.dirty_money_yield = yield
    op.was_detected = false
    db.update_operation(op)

    emit_alert(player.id, "crime_completed", {op_id: op.id, yield: yield, heat_gain: heat_gain})
```

```
function laundering_tick():
    // Called every 5 minutes — checks for completed processes
    completed = db.query("""
        SELECT * FROM laundering_processes
        WHERE status = 'IN_PROGRESS' AND completes_at <= NOW()
    """)

    for each process in completed:
        player = db.get_player(process.player_id)

        // Final detection check at completion
        heat = db.get_heat_score(process.player_id)
        final_detection = detection_check_laundering(process, heat)

        if final_detection:
            // Seize the dirty money being laundered, no clean output
            db.update_laundering(process.id, {status: 'SEIZED'})
            db.increment_heat(process.player_id, 150)
            emit_alert(process.player_id, "laundering_seized", {amount: process.dirty_amount})
        else:
            // Successfully laundered
            db.add_clean_cash(process.player_id, process.clean_amount)
            db.decrement_dirty_money(process.player_id, process.dirty_amount)
            db.update_laundering(process.id, {status: 'COMPLETED'})
            emit_alert(process.player_id, "laundering_complete", {clean_amount: process.clean_amount})
```

### detection_check()

```
function detection_check(player, operation, heat):
    base = 0.02

    heat_mult = {
        'COLD': 1.0,
        'WARM': 1.5,
        'HOT': 2.5,
        'BURNING': 4.0,
        'FUGITIVE': 6.0
    }[heat.level]

    risk_mult = operation.risk_level <= 3 ? 0.5
              : operation.risk_level <= 6 ? 1.0
              : operation.risk_level <= 9 ? 2.0
              : 3.5

    security_reduction = 0
    if player.has_security_layer:
        security_reduction = min(0.40, player.security_layer.protection_rating / 250)

    informant_bonus = heat.informant_active ? 0.50 : 0

    bribe_discount = 0
    if player.recent_bribe_at and (NOW() - player.recent_bribe_at) < 24 hours:
        bribe_discount = 0.30

    probability = base
        * heat_mult
        * risk_mult
        * (1 - security_reduction)
        * (1 + informant_bonus)
        - bribe_discount

    probability = max(0.001, min(0.95, probability))  // clamp: always some risk, never certain

    roll = random(0.0, 1.0)
    operation.detection_roll = roll
    db.update_operation_roll(operation.id, roll)

    return roll < probability
```

```
function season_reset(ending_season_id):
    // Phase 1: Lock the season
    db.update_season(ending_season_id, {status: 'ENDING'})

    // Phase 2: Compute final standings
    final_standings = db.query("""
        SELECT p.id, p.username, p.net_worth
        FROM players p
        WHERE p.season_id = ?
        ORDER BY p.net_worth DESC
    """, ending_season_id)

    // Phase 3: Award meta points (rank-based)
    for i, player in enumerate(final_standings):
        rank = i + 1
        meta_points = calculate_meta_points(rank, final_standings.length)
        // Top 1%: 1000 pts, Top 10%: 500 pts, Top 25%: 200 pts, Rest: 50 pts
        db.increment_meta_points(player.id, meta_points)
        db.append_season_history(player.id, {
            season_id: ending_season_id,
            rank: rank,
            net_worth: player.net_worth,
            achievements: get_player_achievements(player.id, ending_season_id)
        })

    // Phase 4: Save season summary
    db.update_season(ending_season_id, {
        status: 'COMPLETED',
        total_players: final_standings.length,
        top_players: final_standings.slice(0, 100),
        winner_id: final_standings[0].id
    })

    // Phase 5: Create next season record
    new_season = db.create_season({
        season_number: ending_season.season_number + 1,
        started_at: NOW(),
        ends_at: NOW() + 6 months,
        status: 'ACTIVE',
        starting_cash: 5000  // or modified by season variant
    })

    // Phase 6: Wipe season-scoped data (ordered to respect FK constraints)
    db.begin_transaction()
    tables_to_wipe = [
        'laundering_processes', 'criminal_operations', 'dirty_money_balances',
        'heat_scores', 'security_layers', 'trade_contracts', 'market_listings',
        'employees', 'businesses', 'resources'
    ]
    for table in tables_to_wipe:
        db.execute("DELETE FROM ? WHERE season_id = ?", table, ending_season_id)

    // Reset season-scoped player fields
    db.execute("""
        UPDATE players SET
            season_id = ?,
            cash = starting_cash + veteran_bonus_cash,
            net_worth = starting_cash + veteran_bonus_cash,
            business_slots = 1,
            reputation_score = 0,
            alignment = 'LEGAL'
        WHERE id IN (SELECT id FROM players WHERE season_id = ?)
    """, new_season.id, ending_season_id)

    db.commit_transaction()

    // Phase 7: Seed new season's resources and AI market
    seed_season_resources(new_season.id)
    market_refresh(new_season.id)

    emit_broadcast("season_started", {new_season_id: new_season.id, season_number: new_season.season_number})
```
